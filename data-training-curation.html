Curation & Training

What is curation?

Curation is the process of checking whether deposited data are complete, understandable, and reusable. It is not only about storing or "dumping" files. It is about making sure that data can actually be interpreted and used by someone else in the future.

Why use curation in training?

One of the easiest ways to practice preparing a good data deposit is to look at data from the other side, which is the side of the curator. Many repositories curate your data before preserving it for 5 to 20 years. This process is not meant to judge your work, but to ensure that your data remain clear, complete, and usable in the long term.

In this training, participants evaluate someone else’s deposited dataset using a curation form provided by Recherche Data Gouv. You could choose any open data on the repository online or you could bring your own data.

The idea is simple: By reviewing a dataset as a curator would, it's harder for you to miss or forget anything when you publish your data.

The curation form can also be used as a checklist before clicking "publish" on your data.
